* [2025-11-19 Wed] - introduction 
**
Integrating sensory information, responding to behaviorally relevant stimuli, and ignoring 
behaviorally irrelevant stimuli is essential to animal survival.
Goal-directed behaviors require the coordinated activity of mulitple brain regions:
thalamus and sensory cortices process and encode stimuli; prefrontal cortex is involved 
in decision making, planning, and working memory; and neuromodulatory brainstem nuclei 
dynamically modulate processing in other brain regions through attention and arousal.
(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1364661314001570
https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.09.23.677709v1.full 
https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-024-01787-0)
In particular, activity in the locus coeruleus, the brain's primary source of norepinephrine, has been shown
to modulate feedforward processing of behaviorally-relevant stimuli and correlate with performance in goal-directed behaviors
(Hirata et al., 2006; Devilbiss et al., 2006; Rodenkirch et al., 2019; Rajkowski et al., 1994; Usher et al., 1999).
Recent developments in genetically encoded fluorescent sensors in vivo 
have opened a window onto the dynamics of norepinephrine released during sensory processing, 
learned behaviors, and sleep 
(https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/30922875
https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-022-04782-2
https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/35798980
https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/37474807
Liu et al. 2025).

Pupil diameter is a reliable physiological biomarker of arousal and predictor of task performance 
in sensory signal detection tasks (Schriver et al. 2018; Narasimhan et al. 2023).  
There is also strong evidence of a link between neural activity in LC and dynamic 
changes in pupil diameter; however, the relationships between LC activity, pupil area, 
and arousal are strongly nonlinear.  Although large pupil dilations often coincided with 
bursts of LC firing (reflecting arousal or salient stimuli), pupil area alone
is a poor index of spiking activity in LC (Yang et al. 2023).  While arousal is a strong predictor of behavior,
mice exhibit distinct strategies during goal directed behaviors that do not neatly map to arousal level (Ashwood et al. 2022)
The relationship between pupil area and noradrenergic dynamics to those behavioral strategies remains unclear.

Here we explored the relationships between pupil dynamics, noradrenergic dynamics, and behavior in 
a somatosensory signal detection task. Mice were trained to ignore auditory distractors and respond 
to behaviorally relevant tactile signals in pursuit of water rewards. We simultaneous montiored pupil
dynamics and fluorescent GRABNE signals in somatosensory and prefrontal cortices during task execution.
We found that baseline pupil area and baseline synchronization of GRABNE signals were strong predictors of behavior, 
but not baseline cortical GRABNE levels.
We also employed a generalized linear model - hidden Markov model framework developed by Ashwood et al. (2022) to predict 
performance and identify distinct behavioral states. 
We found that baseline pupil area was a better predictor of behavior than task performance on previous trials.
